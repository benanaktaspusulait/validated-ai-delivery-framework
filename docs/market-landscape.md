# Market and Competitive Landscape

Where this framework sits among adjacent tools, how it differs and who it serves. This is positioning analysis, not an endorsement or critique of any named product.

## Adjacent categories

| Category | Examples (illustrative) | What they optimise | Gap this framework addresses |
|---|---|---|---|
| Engineering intelligence / DORA | Delivery-metrics dashboards | Delivery speed and stability | No AI-specific validation cost or review-debt view |
| Code quality / static analysis | SonarQube-style tools, Snyk | Code health and security findings | Measure quality, not the delivery economics of AI assistance |
| Developer productivity analytics | SPACE/DevEx analytics suites | Productivity and developer experience | Risk of individual surveillance; no AI governance lens |
| AI coding assistants | Copilot, Cursor and similar | Generating output faster | They create the output; they do not govern its validated delivery |
| AI governance / model risk | Model-risk and policy tools | Model behaviour and compliance | Focused on models, not on the delivery system that absorbs AI output |

## Differentiation

```text
AI-specific: measures validation cost, review debt, AI-assisted defect linkage and net delivery value, not generic productivity.
Governs the delivery system, not the model: complements coding assistants instead of competing with them.
Psychological safety as a first-class gate: adoption pauses if trust drops, which surveillance-style tools ignore.
Confidence-honest: every estimate carries a Data Confidence Score; it claims decision-grade signals, not causal proof.
Graduated control: observation to warning to confidence-gated enforcement, with an always-available emergency override.
```

## Target segments

| Segment | Primary driver | How the framework lands |
|---|---|---|
| Large enterprise | Governance, audit and risk control over fast AI adoption | Strong fit: policy engine, audit trail, retention, RBAC |
| Fast-growing SaaS | Keep velocity without accumulating hidden review/quality debt | Strong fit: review-debt and net-value signals, light developer touch |
| Public sector / regulated | Compliance and change-resistance management | Fit with care: emphasise privacy basis, slow rollout, observation-first |
| Small teams / startups | Lightweight insight without bureaucracy | Partial fit: free-tier, Observation Mode only until value is proven |

## SWOT

| Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---|---|
| Clear, differentiated thesis; non-punitive design; confidence-honest metrics; staged rollout | Several metrics are estimates, not exact; value depends on disciplined AI metadata; MVP is single-tenant |

| Opportunities | Threats |
|---|---|
| AI adoption outpacing governance; demand for trustworthy AI delivery evidence; open ecosystem of connectors | Incumbents adding AI lenses; organisations misusing data for ranking; "metrics theatre" eroding trust |

## Positioning statement

```text
For platform and engineering leaders adopting AI-assisted delivery, the Validated AI Delivery Framework is a control plane that shows whether AI is creating validated value or hidden review, quality and economic debt, without surveilling individuals or claiming proof it cannot support.
```
