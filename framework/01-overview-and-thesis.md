# Executive Summary and Core Thesis

## AI Delivery Control Plane for Platform Engineering Teams

**Version:** 1.8 | **Status:** Platform-ready | **Audience:** Platform Engineering, DevEx, CTO Office, Security, Engineering Managers

AI-assisted software delivery changes the whole delivery system: code generation, review load, security posture, delivery economics and team behaviour. The main risk is not that AI cannot generate output — it is that AI generates more output than the organisation can safely validate, absorb and maintain.

This framework positions the capability as a **Platform Engineering tool**:

> Platform teams operate it. Engineering managers interpret results. Tech leads act on recommendations. Developers interact with minimal friction. CTO, security and compliance use it for governance and investment decisions.

**It is not an individual developer monitoring system.** It is:

> **An AI Delivery Control Plane that helps engineering organisations measure the real value, risk and validation cost of AI-assisted software delivery.**

## Core Thesis

> AI output speed is not the same as validated delivery capacity.

Validated delivery means AI-assisted output that is understood, reviewed, tested, secure, maintainable, economically beneficial and safe to deploy. The primary metric is not AI output volume — it is validated delivery.

## Product Positioning

```text
AI Delivery Control Plane for Platform Engineering Teams
```

Simple message: *Measure the real value and risk of AI-assisted software delivery.*

## What This Product Is Not

```text
Not surveillance — we help teams understand delivery value, not track individuals.
Not an AI assistant — it measures delivery impact of tools like Copilot.
Not just DORA — adds AI-specific dimensions: validation cost, review debt, trust calibration.
Not a static checklist — thresholds adapt by team maturity, defects and review capacity.
```
